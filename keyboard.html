Space out notes by typing / slashes between them, each one adding an extra beat: e//g plays an e note, leaves two beats, then plays a g note. Use this to create both long notes and musical pauses - they both sound similar enough.


Squash notes together by typing one or more \ backslashes after a letter. Each \ cuts its note in half, so e\g plays a half-beat or 'quaver' e followed by a normal length g, whereas e\\g plays a shorter quarter-beat or 'semiquaver' e, and then the normal length 'crotchet' g.


If you need a 'dotted' note, use e\/ which is half a beat plus a beat - the length of three half beats. If you need a double dot, then e\/\/ is seven quarter beats - the 'dotted' note described before cut in half by the second \ to make three quarter beats (a dotted quaver), and the final / adding one beat - same as four additional quarter beats.


You can change the speed of the WHOLE tune by putting several / and \ tweaks at the VERY start of your piece (before any notes). This sets the tune speed as if those tweaks were placed after every note.


If you need more notes, the lower keys z-m work, and shift makes them lower. Also, press [this] to shuffle your turn higher and [this] to shuffle it lower.
